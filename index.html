What is going on?

Over one million Uighurs have been interned by the Chinese government and sent to concentration camps within Xinjiang since 2017. The government has detention camps set up in which the Muslim minority group, Uighurs, are suppressed and tortured.

The people of the Uighur community are taken and interrogated and beaten because of their religion. In these camps, they are forced to do things that are against their religion such as consume alcohol, eat pork, and pledge to convert to another religion.

Nearly a hundred camps have been identified in the Northwest China region where the Uighurs have been detained. The government refers to at "re-education centers" trying to conceal the human rights violation that is going on.

Who are the Uighurs and why are they being targeted?

There are nearly 11 million Uighur Muslims who reside in Xinjiang. The land in which they live in was previously called East Turkestan. In 1949 it was annexed, and since then Xinjiang has been under control of China.

In the past Uighur militants were involved in atttacks in China. China usese these attacks to claim that Uighurs hold extremist views and as a whole they have the potential to be a great threat to the country's security.

Beginning in 2017, the Xinjiang government began to place restrictions on things that have been associated with Muslims. Men were prohibited from growing long beards as women were refrained from wearing headscarfs. Not only were people being restricted, but mosques began to be destructed signifying the destruction of the Muslim religion.
